Canonical analysis

partially supported / overstated▶ 00:07:30

“Josephus records that more than 1 million Jewish people starved to death in the Roman siege of Jerusalem (70 AD).”

Pastor's claim (full)

Josephus records that more than 1 million Jewish people starved to death in the Roman siege of Jerusalem (70 AD).

Evidence the pastor cited

'Josephus the first century celebrated historian records…' — author named, no work or passage citation.

What the evidence shows

Josephus (Jewish War VI.9.3) does give a figure of approximately 1.1 million killed during the siege. The qualifiers Hagee drops matter: (1) Josephus does not say all 1.1 million starved — his figure includes combat deaths, crucifixions, massacre, disease, and starvation; (2) Josephus is notoriously prone to inflated casualty figures; modern historians (Seth Schwartz, Martin Goodman) estimate actual Jerusalem population at the time was likely 70,000–100,000 (swelling during Passover), with siege-era deaths estimated in the tens of thousands to low hundreds of thousands. So: Josephus did write what Hagee says, but the number is historically contested and Hagee narrows the cause to 'starvation' specifically, which is an overstatement.

Sources consulted

  • Josephus, The Jewish War, Book VI, ch. 9, §3 (Project Gutenberg)
  • Martin Goodman — Rome and Jerusalem: The Clash of Ancient Civilizations (Penguin, 2007)
