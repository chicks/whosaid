Reading current political leaders as decoded biblical types

Topic: Whether Christians should identify modern political figures as fulfilling OT king-templates
Why flagged: Decoding named contemporary political figures as precise OT-king types is the kind of move Jesus refused, the prophetic books warn against (Jer 23, Deut 18), and apocalyptic literature does not actually do (its referents are the writer's own contemporaries, not figures 2,500 years in the writer's future). The hermeneutical engine of the entire sermon has no canonical license.

Canonical analysis

The Bible contains several different stances on decoding political leaders. (1) Jesus's stance: not your business, withdraw from sign-decoding, beware false 'here he is!' identifications (Acts 1:7, Matt 24:23–24, John 6:15). (2) The classical prophetic stance (Jeremiah, Amos, Isaiah): name and confront the actual king in front of you, in his lifetime, by name, with concrete moral content — *not* identify him as a re-incarnation of an earlier king. (3) Apocalyptic stance (Daniel, Revelation): use coded animal/numerical imagery to refer to the empire of the writer's own day, not centuries ahead. (4) Wisdom stance (Eccl): patterns recur generally; specifying exact correspondences is exactly what Eccl is *not* doing. The sermon's framing — that modern leaders are precise channels of named ancient kings, with year-counts, date-counts, and event-counts validating the identification — has no canonical home. Deut 18:22 and Jer 23 both supply the test: a prophetic decoding that requires constant goal-shifting (the 28-year span includes a hypothetical 2028 inauguration; if Trump leaves office earlier or later the count fails) is the kind of decoding the canon explicitly warns against.
