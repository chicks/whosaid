Summary

Preached on the fifth Wednesday of the U.S. COVID-19 lockdown (April 29, 2020), Locke walks verse-by-verse through 2 Timothy 3:1–5, reading each of Paul's listed vices as present-tense signs of the imminent rapture. Along the way he (1) reinterprets 'without natural affection' as maternal abortion, (2) mocks mask-wearing and equates masked Christians with spiritual 'fakes' during an active respiratory pandemic, (3) references people 'burning the streets of our city down,' (4) prescribes cutting 'toxic' people out of congregants' lives, and (5) closes by urging listeners to 'put on the entire armour of God because we are in a battle.' The sermon is framed throughout as sign-reading the present moment as the countdown to Christ's return.

severeAxis 4▶ 00:05:00Headline

End-times sign-reading as a preaching genre

Topic: Whether Christians should read current events as end-times signs
Why flagged: The dominant canonical witness on the timing of Christ's return is 'you will not know; do not try to know.' The sermon's entire frame — 'we are watching it leap off the page… black and white before our very eyes' — runs opposite to this witness and is not reconciled with Matt 24:36, one chapter before the passage Locke alludes to at his close ('oil in the lamp').

Canonical analysis

Every major canonical voice on the timing of Christ's return converges: it is unknowable, sudden, thief-like. Jesus disclaims the knowledge himself (Matt 24:36), refuses signs (Matt 16:4), tells the disciples at the ascension it's not theirs to know (Acts 1:7), and uses Noah specifically to make the point that the flood was UNEXPECTED. Paul uses the 'thief in the night' image. Peter, answering Locke's exact rhetorical situation — scoffers saying it's been too long — pushes back not by pointing to signs but by saying God is patient and operates on a different time scale. Revelation adds the same thief-image aimed at complacent churches. The whole-Bible picture is a warning against the sign-reading posture, not for it. Locke's method — mapping a first-century vice list onto the April 2020 news cycle and declaring the Bible 'being fulfilled moment by moment black and white before our very eyes' — is precisely what 2 Peter 3 anticipated and pushed back against.

Why flagged: The dominant canonical witness on how to treat difficult, sinful, or hostile people is patient engagement, restoration, and love-of-enemy. Canonical analysis

And Jesus's dominant pattern — eating with sinners, going after the lost sheep, loving enemies, blessing persecutors — is the exact opposite of a general 'toxic people' cut-off rule. Locke's generalization skips all the canonical guardrails.

“Abortions are at an all-time high (U.S., as of April 2020).”

Pastor's claim (full)

Abortions are at an all-time high (U.S., as of April 2020).

Evidence the pastor cited

None. Delivered as a standalone explanatory statistic inside the 'without natural affection' exposition.

What the evidence shows

As of April 2020 the U.S. abortion rate and abortion ratio were near historic lows, not at an all-time high. The Guttmacher Institute's most recent data point before this sermon (2017) reported a U.S. abortion rate of 13.5 per 1,000 women aged 15–44 — the lowest rate recorded since Roe v. Wade (1973), when the rate was 16.3 and rising through the early 1980s peak near 29.3 (1981). The CDC's Abortion Surveillance report for 2018 (published 2020) confirmed continued decline through 2018. The 'all-time high' framing is false under both major U.S. data sources (Guttmacher and CDC) on the rate, the ratio, and the absolute-number metrics for any post-1980 denominator.

There are only five places in the New Testament where God says 'this is the will of God,' and 1 Thess 5:18 is one of them.

Evidence the pastor cited

None. Delivered as a count.

What the evidence shows

The phrase 'the will of God' (thelēma tou theou / θέλημα τοῦ θεοῦ) occurs 20+ times in the Greek NT in various grammatical forms (e.g., Matt 7:21; Mark 3:35; Rom 1:10; 12:2; Eph 1:1; Col 1:1; Heb 10:36; 1 Pet 2:15). What Locke likely has in mind is a narrower construction — passages using the formula 'this is the will of God' (touto estin thelēma theou) applied to a specific behavioral command. Under that narrower lens, popular evangelical lists cite four to five: 1 Thess 4:3 (sanctification), 1 Thess 5:18 (thanksgiving), 1 Pet 2:15 (doing good silences foolish critics), 1 Pet 4:19 (entrusting one's soul to God in suffering), and sometimes Eph 5:17–18 (being filled with the Spirit). The claim is defensible under that restricted construction, but Locke's unqualified framing ('only five times in the whole Bible') is overstated.

Pastor's claim (full)

The chameleon 'can basically change colors with its environment' — i.e., chameleons camouflage by matching their surroundings.

Evidence the pastor cited

Personal anecdote from a trip to Ghana.

What the evidence shows

This is a common popular misconception. Peer-reviewed research (Stuart-Fox & Moussalli 2008, PLoS Biology; Teyssier et al. 2015, Nature Communications) shows chameleons primarily change color for social signaling (dominance displays, mating) and thermoregulation, not to match their background. Color change is driven by active tuning of guanine nanocrystals in specialized iridophore cells, not by background matching. Some species exhibit limited background-matching in specific contexts, but 'change colors with its environment' reverses the actual primary function. Minor to the theological argument but illustrative of evidence hygiene.

Pastor's claim (full)

'It took him four thousand years to show up the first time' — i.e., roughly 4,000 years between creation and the first advent of Christ.

Evidence the pastor cited

None. Delivered as a rhetorical aside.

What the evidence shows

The '4,000 years from Adam to Christ' figure derives from Archbishop James Ussher's 17th-century chronology (Annales Veteris Testamenti, 1650), which dated creation to 4004 BC by summing Masoretic genealogies. Ussher's dating is a specific textual tradition, not a consensus figure. Mainstream geology and cosmology place Earth's age at ~4.54 billion years and the emergence of anatomically modern humans at ~300,000 years ago. Even within biblical scholarship, the Septuagint (LXX) chronology yields ~5,500 years between creation and Christ rather than 4,000. Delivered without qualification, the figure assumes a young-earth framework the congregation is not told it is assuming.
