whosaid · a sermon audit project

Sermon Audits

A structured audit of modern American sermons measured against four independent axes: fidelity to Jesus's teachings in the Gospels, bias-bearing or harmful rhetoric, factual accuracy of empirical claims, and whole-Bible engagement (cherry-picking and unacknowledged canonical tension).

Source & methodology: github.com/chicks/whosaid

This is a methodology experiment.
  • Each audit is produced by a structured AI-assisted pipeline (Claude Opus 4.7) with explicit rubrics. The rubrics, system prompt, and raw findings JSONs are all in the source repository linked below — full methodological transparency.
  • Pastor quotes are pulled from YouTube auto-captions; minor transcription errors are possible. The underlying video is linked from every report for verification.
  • Each audit characterizes a single specific sermon, not the pastor or church in general.
  • Methodology disputes, counter-examples, and rubric suggestions welcome — email charles@reliable.cx.

Methodology

Each sermon is audited along four axes:

  • Axis 1 — Fidelity to Jesus's Teachings (Red Letters). The pastor's claims are compared against Jesus's direct words in the four Gospels, including OT passages Jesus explicitly cited or interpreted (e.g., Lev 19:18 via Matt 22:39, Jonah via Matt 12:39–41). Flagged findings fall into three categories: contradicted, tension, or notably absent.
  • Axis 2 — Identifiable Biases / Harmful Rhetoric. Co-equal sub-rubrics for Christian nationalism (Whitehead & Perry's Taking America Back for God; Du Mez, Jesus and John Wayne; Alberta, The Kingdom, the Power, and the Glory), anti-Muslim, anti-LGBTQ, misogynistic, and anti-Jewish framings. Severity weighted by reach × specificity × real-world harm potential.
  • Axis 3 — Factual Claims & Evidence Check. Empirical claims extracted from the sermon are compared against independent scholarly, journalistic, and primary sources. Verdict scale: Supported / Partially supported / Contested / Misleading / Unsupported / False / Needs verification.
  • Axis 4 — Whole-Bible Engagement. For each major framing in the sermon, the pastor's claim is placed side-by-side with what Jesus said in the Gospels and what the wider Bible says (OT + NT non-Gospel). Surfaces cherry-picking and unacknowledged canonical tension.

What each axis score means

Low (low) — Few findings or isolated tensions; sermon is largely aligned on this axis.
Moderate (moderate) — Multiple substantive findings, or a directional concern without extreme content.
Severe (severe) — Content with significant reach × specificity × harm potential; high-confidence assertions targeting identifiable groups or populations.

Evidence-first format

Every flagged finding includes the pastor's direct quote with a timestamp, and the specific biblical passages it sits against. Contrast is two-panel (Axis 1: Pastor | Jesus) or three-panel (Axis 4: Pastor | Jesus | Wider Bible). The audit's credibility depends on the evidence doing the work, not the auditor's prose.

Pipeline: yt-dlp (transcript) → cleaner → Claude Opus 4.7 (structured findings via JSON Schema) → HTML/PDF generator. Each sermon audit runs ~$1 and ~5 min of model time. Reach data from YouTube + public megachurch attendance figures.

Rubrics: Whitehead & Perry 2020; Du Mez 2020; Alberta 2023. Gospel quotations: ESV.

Source repository: github.com/chicks/whosaid — all prompts, rubrics, findings JSONs, and pipeline scripts are published there.
